Matthew 1:1

Contextual Background

These verse introduces us to two different human parties, Herod and the mysterious Magi. In other to understand the rest of the chapter it is important to have a good understanding about these two. Let us analyze them separately:

Herod

His Background

The Herodian Dynasty was a difficult one, filled with blood, incest, pride, greed and more. The dynasty actually comes into historical existence during the time of Herod's grandfather, Antipater I. Antipater I was a native of Idumea [descendants of Edom (Esau Gen. 25:30)] {1} who became governor of that region when Idumea was conquered by John Hyrcanus I {2} , son of Simon the Hasmonean of the family known as the Maccabees. Upon the death of Hyrcanus I and later the death of his son and heir to the throne, Judah Aristobulus I, Alexander Jannaeus, brother of Aristobulus I, succeeded him in royal power and high priesthood office, for this was the custom of the Hasmoneans, that whoever occupied the royal title also fulfilled the role of the High Priest. A practice contrary to the Old Testament custom. Old Testament kings, Saul and Uzziah paid a high price for attempting to usurp the duties of the priest. Alexander Jannaeus was succeeded by his younger son Judas Aristobulus II, who had ousted his older brother, John Hyrcanus II, the rightful heir to the throne.

At this point, the son of the now deceased Antipater I, Antipater II (Herod's father) moved to the side of the weaker Hasmonean brother, John Hyrcanus II. When Syria was conquered by the Roman conqueror Pompey in 64 B.C. the Hasmonean rulership came to an end. however, Pompey gave back the High Priesthood office to Hyrcanus II and the rule of the new Roman province to Antipater II. When Julius Caesar and Pompey came in conflict and Pompey was defeated by Julius Caesar, Hyrcanus II and Herod’s father, Antipater II, moved to the side of Julius Caesar. Herod’s father, according to tradition, risked his life for Julius Caesar during a fight in Egypt, and thus he was made a Roman citizen and procurator of Judea {3}. Consequently, Antipater II appointed Herod, his second son, as governor of Galilee at the age of 25 in 47 B.C. {4}.

The time in history which Matthew is narrating is said to be somewhere around 5 B.C., during the reign of the first Roman Emperor Gaius Octavian, better known as Caesar Augustus, adoptive son of Julius Caesar. In 41 B.C. Mark Anthony convinced Octavian to appoint Herod as king over Parthian-occupied Judea. {5} Herod's diplomatic qualities and his incredible loyalty to those he served gained him the title "The Great" and stablished this Idumean dynasty in history as the Herods rather than the Antipatrids, after his father's and grandfather’s name {6}.

The Young Herod

When Idumea, Herod’s birth place, was conquered by the Hasmonean, John Hyrcanus I, the people willingly took on the Jewish culture. Herod, growing up peculiarly close to the High Priesthood leadership, fell in love with the Jewish traditions and became a Sadducee himself {7}. Matthew 22:15-16 possibly alludes to this.

Herod had been made king over Judea, but he was not conformed with being seen as king by the Judeans; he wanted to be seen as a Jew and he made everything within the power of his hands to be accepted as a such even marrying the High Priest’s daughter, Mariamme I. Nevertheless, the Jews never quite accept him as a Jew; to them he was only a half idummean, causing him to live with an inferiority complex all his life.

The young Herod is described by history as loyal, athletic, politically skilled, ambitious, and incredibly loyal to those he served.

The Decline of His Character

His quality of character remained normal, in terms of a political leader at his time, until the accumulative occurrence of several events in his life caused his psychological decline. Most of these events can be attributed to his closest relatives, as his sister Salome who poisoned his mind against Mariamme I, his favorite wife, and her two sons, ending with the murder of all three of them at the account of Herod himself. His oldest son, Antipater, also contributed to the count of events that would later cause his frenzies by attempting to usurp the throne a few times.

Nevertheless, it is the belief of many historians that the steep decline of his sanity can be attributed to one particular event: his murdering of his favorite wife Mariamme I. All sanity seems to have left him afterwards {8}. He became Schizophrenic; afraid that everyone was after his throne and did not hesitate to get out of the way any possible rivals. It was this kind of Herod the Magi approached when they came to bow down the true King of the Jews. No wonder why such news provoked him to take the measures he did.

The Maggi

The bible remains silent even about the most basic details of these men, therefore, most conclusion we can reach might not cross the borders of mere speculation. Although they have been assigned the names of Gaspar, Melchior, and Balthasar, it is believed that these names can be traced down to Origen in the 3rd century and eventually popularized during the 6th century {9}. The mystery of their names, however, is not the most intricate of the details. Their origin It has been stated by many that the fact that Matthew is silent about their exact place of indicates that he also was unaware of their origin, since he introduces them as “men from the east” (Matthew 2:1).

However, Francis W. Upham has made an outstanding job in deciphering the geographical area in scope as being Persia. He argues that it is not that Matthew did not know the place of origin of these personalities, but rather that the phrase “far east” was so commonly attached to that specific region at his time that it could be referenced without the danger of confusion by his audience {10}.

In the same way we use the terms as “South” or “West Coast” in America to signal particular geographical areas of the country, so we see these languages being used by the prophets of the Old Testament to reference a particular area relative to the land of Israel. The four cardinal directions became even more specific after the exile of Israel and Judah. Assyria became the North and Babylonia became known as the East (Ezekiel 24:4; Isaiah 2:6; {11}.

Upham argues that the presence of the word east in the greek as ἀνατολῶν in verse one and as ἀνατολῇ in verse two together with its repetition should change the translation to the first east to “Far East” instead {12} and thus, since it is “Far East” and not “East”, Matthew must be referencing a land further away than Babylon, and Persia seems to be the most plausible option. This seems a bit of a stretch. The change in grammatical case, from ἀνατολῶν to ἀνατολῇ, in the word east is due to the preposition that precedes them: ἀπὸ (from) and ἐν (in) respectively. Each preposition changes the grammatical case of the noun that follows them, but it is hard to see how their change in grammatical case would cause their meaning to change at all.

Nevertheless, it is true, as he points out, that the phrase “...from the east” twice appearing in Isaiah (46:11, 41:2), means Persia, as the one “from the east” being referred to is Cyrus the Persian in these two instances {13}.

The statement by Matthew that the men went “their own country by another way” (Matthew 2:12) does allude to the idea that Matthew knew their citizenship and perhaps was aware of the common route through Damascus typically traveled by the Easterners visiting Judea {14}. That was a common route for traders and Jewish proselytes who came to Jerusalem to the feasts of the Jews as seen in Acts 2:9. There is, therefore, very convincing proof that these men were natives from Persia or Babylon.

The Magi in Babylon and Medo-Persia

Concluding the origin of these men much facilitates the deciphering of their character. In terms of character Matthew introduces these men, as Magi. That these pilgrims were Persian/ Babylonian and were also Magi, resounds with the record found in the book of Daniel (Daniel 2:2), as he makes mention of an office at the service of Nebuchadnezzar, called the Magi, using the same word as Matthew "μάγους" (Septuagint).

These Magi appear in history as renown scholars of the ancient world; well-learned in science, history, mathematics and the occult. The word Magi is said to originate from Zoroastrianism. The earliest appearance of the word may be traced back to a Zendavesta hymn, used in the religion of Zoroastrianism {15}. Thus, these magi most probably were a priestly order of Zoroastrianism {16}.

When Babylon fell to the Mede and the Persians, their office in the kings court was not ended, but rather elevated since magian Zoroastrianism was reinstated as the state religion of the Medo-Persian empire in the 3rd century B. C. One of the new privileges that came with their role was to assist in the election and deposition of the Monarch {17}, thus rising to prominence.

Origins of Zoroastrianism

The character of the Maggi can be better understood when taking a look into the origins of Zoroastrianism. Alexander Hislop, in his work the two Babylons stated that the name derives from two ancient Chaldean terms "Zara," meaning seed, which indeed is used in Genesis 3:15, and "Astha" or "Isha" meaning woman. Therefore the term "Zoroaster" can be translated as "the seed of the woman." {18}

He also claims that this character referred to as Zoroaster was none the less than Nimrod (Genesis 10:8) {19}, who is attributed the invention of the magic arts and the first one to practice a deep study of the heavenly bodies and the occult. A theory that does not sound all to wild when taking into consideration that it was Nimrod that first tried to reach for heavens (Genesis 10:9-10; 11:1-9), taking after the nature of his father the devil who attempted to do the same thing (Isaiah 14:12-15).

It is not debate among most students of the Bible that Nimrod is an antitype of the Antichrist. Thus, if he indeed took the title of Zoroaster, “The seed of the woman”, he did it in aim to set himself as the redeemer, thus attempting to usurp the role of the True Seed of the woman, our Lord Jesus Christ.

This theory accumulates more credibility when taking into consideration that Babylon was stablished by Nimrod himself (Genesis 10:9). Is not too hard, therefore, to reconcile the idea that the "μάγους" (magi) from Daniel were priests from Zoroastrianism and that by the time of Nebuchadnezzar their priestly order had been already well established, for it has also been stated by historians that it was Zoroaster who stablished the Chaldean Maggi {20}. Although many other historians find the first appearances of Zoroastrianism in Persia and Mede, it is more probable this office had its beginnings in among the Chaldees in Babylon and one way or another might have spread to the surrounding regions as people were sparse from Babylon (Genesis 11:9)

The Magi and Daniel

Taking into consideration that Zoroastrianism did indeed have its beginning in Nimrod, and that him, knowing the promise of a Redeeming Seed from the woman (Genesis 15 3:15), a revelation no doubt passed down from Adam to Noah and from Noah to its descendants, he choose to take that role upon himself.

Hislop goes on to demonstrate how upon Nimrods death he became deified into Orion and placed among the stars. {21} In these way he became the seed and the star in Zoroastrianism. {22}

Daniel came in contact with the Magi when he was introduced to the court of Nebuchadnezzar (Daniel 1:3-6). Although the Bible gives us no information on the relationship between Daniel and the Magi, from his godly and pleasant character, it is hard to doubt that he was able to establish a friendly and close relationship with these learned men that he had to interact with quite often. After all, he had also saved their very lives. This elite group of scholars in Babylon seems to have been divided into 4 groups: the magicians, the astrologers, the sorcerers, and the Chaldeans (Daniel 2:2), however, it is clear that the group in its entirety could be addressed as “the wise man” (Daniel 2:48). They were divided perhaps because each subdivision had a particular area of study. The role of Daniel In the court of Babylon passed from being a student to being the ruler of the wise man, which implied being their leader not only in governance but in teaching, since it was their role of these man to be in continuous study ad pursuit of wisdom. They were the research team of the kingdom. The NASA of Babylon. (Daniel 2:48). No doubt these wise men grew in admiration of their leader and his great God- given wisdom. One may ask, if he had such great relationship and influence among them, how is it they set him up into the Lion’s den? But it must be noted that it was not this elite group of scholars who set him up, but a group known as the administrators and the satraps. A group of vile, greedy, power hungry men with no regard for Daniel nor his God (Daniel 6:4-5). It is quite probable, therefore, that Daniel made use of every opportunity to witness to those around him, and specially those under his care, about his great God.

As he became more acquainted with their religion, he used common ground to introduce them to the the true religion. These Zoroastrian priests worshiped a seed who would one day redeem humanity from evil {23}. Daniel knew the True Seed. They claimed one of the ways these seed represented himself was through a star. Daniel knew the True Star who would rise out of Jacob (Numbers 24:17). It is very easily how 16 Daniel could find common ground with these man to introduce them to his God. A method that the apostle Paul himself used (Acts 17:16-32).

Although we have seen that there was a group among the wise men known as the sorcerers and other as the enchanters, it is known from history, as we have seen, that the Zoroastrian priests were the first ones to practice it, as they had received it from Zoroaster. Sorcery in its wildest term is the science of the occult {24} therefore, we can conclude that the Magi were not only of priestly and royal character, but also plain sorcerers. We can bolster this claim by recalling Simon the sorcerer (Acts 8:9) who is described by Luke using the same term.

Spiritual Application

Jesus was born in Bethlehem of Judea, a small town located less than two hours away from Jerusalem at a walking speed heart of Judea where you could find the temple, a group of elite Law Scholars called the Scribes, another group of doctorate level theologians called the pharisees and yet another group of political-religious leaders known as as the Sadducees, who also occupied the High Priesthood.

The temple was the hope and pride of Judea (Mark 13:1) and and these groups of individuals its boast. Jerusalem was where every sinner ran to obtain forgiveness from God; where those who had not been born in a Jewish family came to know God and about God (Arts 8:27) the temple was where you dedicated your children (Luke 2:22) and you came to be pronounced healed (Mark 1:44); where you came to hear the right theology and where you exercised the right worship (John 4:20). To put it in modern terms, in Jerusalem you had the most educated, eloquent, and popular preachers. That's where you had the most well known Torah pop singers.

Nonetheless, in exception to the close relatives and friends of Mary and Joseph, nobody had a clue of what was occurring. God had come into flesh (John 1:14) and none of the scholars, theologians, priests or common temple folks knew about it. Herod, the biggest charity giver among the Jews and a Sadducee himself, who out of his own pocket had paid to beautify the temple, was totally ignorant about it. Yet, here we have this group of Magi, from the darkest of Moral and Spiritual Backgrounds, who traveled a distance not less than 550 miles to get to Jerusalem {25}. A journey that could have taken anywhere 17 from 6 weeks {26} to four months as it took Ezra (Ezra 7:9). it was to them that it was revealed what was happening.

What a shock! When the Messiah ever came it was the scholars, the priests, the theologians, and the prestigious who were supposed to be the first ones to find out (verse 4) and to believe in Him (Jon 7:47), not a group of pagan sinners and vile sorcerers.

Oh, if God ever revealed His truth it had to be to the preacher with a doctorate in Theology or the one popular with the crowds. The one who gets featured in the newest pop news and gets invited to the trendy talk shows of the moment. If God had truth to reveal it had to be to the churchy individual who has had a perfect church attendance for over 20, 30 or 40 years. The one who was born on Saturday and came to their first church service on Sunday. If God had truth to reveal it had to be to the most talented, best looking musician, Christian pop singer who can fill a football arena or attract the whole town to the church. The kind of musician/ singer that if he does not show up at church, service nobody makes it to the altar. Surely that is the kind of crowd that God wanted to work with. The group He just has to use because oh my, they are just too talented and qualified to pass them by.

But this group of sinners? How can He use a drug addict living under a bridge that does not even know which is the first book of the bible? How can that prostitute on the streets who has been abused, rejected, and despised and know more beds than a hotel maid ever be of any use to Him? How can that dirty drunkard who has destroyed his family, reputation life ever step into God’s plan? How can God use a drug dealer, a murderer, or a crooked business man? Surely He was not about to pass over a group of such refined churched folks for a group no-gooders? But He did.

This verses teaches us that God seeks the hungry not the popular, intelligent, talented or influential. You are not necessary to God’s work my friend. You can be replaced. God can indeed pass you over. If you don’t want to do the will of God; if you refuse to allow Him to work in your life and change your heart; If you refuse to walk after His plan for your life because you just cannot let go of certain things in your life, do not worry. He’s already arranging everything in the life of a 7 year-old kid who’s kicking needles of meth in the streets at midnight because he was abandoned by his own mother so that he will come to His feet, repent, get clean and take your place. He’s already got a lady in a 18 dark room, crying to sleep every night and thinking about ending her life because she thinks there is mo more hope or meaning for her to come and do the work you think you are too good for to do.

He is already got someone who might be in the worst spiritual state but that is wiling to abandon the sins you are not.

We should not be amazed that God used a group of sorcerers instead of the scribes, the pharisees, and the sadducees. After all, these group of religious men were also sorcerers, only that of a different kind, for the sin of disobedience is like the sin of witchcraft (1 Samuel 12:23). Do not be surprised if god passes you over by the new convert at church who is willing to live the holy life you are not.

What God was really doing was replacing one sorcerer by another sorcerer. Only that one group of sorcerers was willing to acknowledge their low sinful state, while the other group thought themselves perfect.

We cannot be of any use of God until we acknowledge that we are nothing but hopeless sinners hanging on the strings of His grace and Mercy and are willing to walk in His truth and will.
