Matthew 1:3

Contextual Background

The Arrival of the Magi

That πᾶσα Ἱεροσόλυμα (all Jerusalem) was terrified along with Herod produces at least two theories:

  1. Herod's court failed to keep the secret
  2. The coming of the Magi was a pompous entrance

Herod's court failed to keep the secret

They came to Jerusalem indiscreetly. When Herod found out the purpose of their visit, he adverted his court about not telling anyone, but they somehow failed to keep it a secret. This does not make for a horrible theory but it is very improbable, nevertheless. The Magi brought terrible news for Herod and his dynasty; it is unthinkable he would have let this become public, specially when considering how much Herod cared for the public's opinion. Josephus records he placed spies everywhere and at times he would even disguise himself among the public and find out what they thought about his reign and punish those who disagreed with him (Antiquities of the Jews XV. 5 4). It is safe to assume, hence, that Herod and his court weren't the only ones to know about their arrival.

The coming of the Magi was a pompous entrance

The most credible theory of the two is that the entry of the Magi to Jerusalem was not incognito. Even if we hold true that they came incognito and that Herod's court failed to keep this visit a secret, it is hard to reconcile how could they have disperse the news to all Jerusalem so fast. They could not grab their latest iPhone and and direct message their close circle to tell them about it. It would have taken days for the news to propagate in πᾶσα Ἱεροσόλυμα (all Jerusalem).

Jerusalem was the most important city of the Roman province, Judea. Although the size of the population in Jerusalem during this period is unknown many historians agree that the population of Jerusalem can be extrapolated to about 30,000 2 (around 500 people per hectare based on Archeological findings) 3.

Spreading the word among a population of that size with the methods available during that time would take a while. The best explanation is that the Magi arrived probably during the day. It is hard to think they would had bothered Herod by arriving late at night. Thus, everyone in the streets had an opportunity to see them enter the city. The fact that Jerusalem entered in great fear at the visit of these man tells us something about the number and the appearance of these men. The safest bet is to assume they were not three in number as is popularly believed and that they were quite pompous in appearance. It is hard to think all Jerusalem would have frightened for the arrival of 3 average-looking men.

Herod the Religious Man

Having heard Herod, he was disturbed. If he was indeed a Sadducee as it is commonly believed 4 and hinted by the scriptures (Matthew 22:15), it is not too hard to believe that he was familiar with the Messianic prophecy. We are also able to gather from Acts 26, and specially from verse 26, that the Herodians were not ignorant to the Jewish beliefs and customs. Therefore, when the Magi came to him he knew exactly what they were talking about.

Spiritual Application

Many know just enough about Jesus to keep them from knowing Jesus

Herod heard but he never listened . He was familiar with the prophecy of the Messiah but not with the Messiah Himself, and thus, failed to recognize Him. In other words, Herod knew about Jesus, but he did not know Jesus.

How tragic this is and how often we see this taking place in our day. Religious people who know much about God but they never have had a real fellowship with God. They show up to church every week but never have stepped into God's throne (Hebrews 4:16). They can hear a sermon every week but have never heard God's voice. (Matthew 13:14-15). They read the Bible but have never let the bible read themselves (Hebrews 4:12). They sign hymns but have never worshipped (Matthew 15: 7- 9). Herod was this kind of man: the religious man; he was okay with Jesus as long as Jesus did not get on his way , once Jesus presented a threat to his throne, then a problem arose.

The religious person enjoys religion. Oh! how they love to boast about their church, pastor, their giving, and their perfect attendance, but yet have never let Jesus Christ reign. They claim Christ as their king but treat Him as if He was their water boy or a plan B. They run to Him only when they need something done or when option A has failed. You cannot say you serve Christ and do whatever you please. Christ will not accept any other position in your life that is not that of King and Lord.

Herod would've been fine if Christ had come to be King of the Egyptians, King of the Babylonians, or King of the Syrians, but he could never permit Him to be βασιλεὺς τῶν Ἰουδαίων ("King of the Jews"), for that would have meant the giving up of his place as king. The religious man and woman are okay, as a matter of fact, boast about people being baptized and giving their life to Christ but never have done it themselves. When Christ knocks at your heart's door He comes for 100% of it. Not for 50%, 75%, or even 99%, but for 100% (Deuteronomy 6:5) and you only have two options, you either reject Him or you accept Him. You let Him live and crucify yourself (Galatians 2:20) or you crucify Him all anew and keep your petty reign which ultimately will lead you to death anyway. Which one will you chose?
